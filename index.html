IRS Standard Deduction
📋 First time here? How to use this calculator — takes about 2 minutes to set up
1
Choose your filing status
Select Single / MFS or Married Filing Jointly using the tabs above.
2
Enter your income details
Fill in your Annual Gross Salary, Standard Deduction, Pay Period, and your current per-period tax withheld (from your pay stub).
3
Fill in the Tax Bracket Table
This is required for results to appear. Visit the IRS bracket page ↗, find your tax year and filing status, then enter the lower limit for each bracket (12% through 35%). The 10% row is pre-filled. The 37% row is optional.
4
Read your results
Once all fields are complete, results populate automatically — tax obligation, take-home pay, effective rate, and your withholding position (whether you're on track, over, or under for the year).
5
Use the Simulator (optional)
In the Pay Period Impact section, enter an additional amount in the Withholding Simulator to see how increasing your withholding affects your position and estimated refund.
⚠️
Federal Income Tax Only
Results reflect federal income tax only. Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), state income tax, state disability insurance, and benefit deductions are not included. Your actual take-home pay will be lower once these are factored in.
ℹ️
IRS Estimator Variance is Normal
This calculator uses the Form 1040 tax bracket method. The IRS Withholding Estimator uses a different payroll method (Pub. 15-T), so a variance of $0–$100 is expected. A larger gap almost always means the wrong tax year's brackets were entered.
